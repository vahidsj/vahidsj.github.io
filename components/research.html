RESEARCH

At the broadest level, my research focuses on human-centered intelligent conversational systems. I believe in task-driven research; my approach to research is to intergrate theory, experimentation, and application to advance both scientific understanding and real-world impact.

OVERARCHING RESEARCH THEMES:

  • Narrative Intelligence in LLMs

    Narratives are central to how humans make sense of information, reason about the world, and communicate complex ideas. By applying storytelling to contexts such as scientific explanation and information-seeking dialogues, I aim to explore narratives as a representational and reasoning framework for LLMs, enabling them to structure information, explain phenomena, and engage users more effectively.

  • The Social Mind of AI Agents

    Human interaction is inherently social, shaped by personality, goals, and context. In this research area, I aim to investigate how LLMs can model, simulate, and adapt to social behaviors in interactive scenarios. This involves studying how traits influence goal achievement and how agents negotiate, collaborate, or persuade within social contexts. The ultimate goal is to move beyond transactional dialogue systems toward socially aware agents that more closely reflect the richness of human communication.

  • Subjective Alignment
    in Conversational Systems

    Conversations extend beyond objective facts, often reflecting opinions, values, and personal preferences. In this research area, I aim to incorporate subjectivity into conversational systems by modeling and aligning with diverse human perspectives. My work explores how such systems can recognize, represent, and adapt to individual preferences and viewpoints, enabling opinion-driven dialogue and fostering more personalized, context-sensitive interactions.

  • Human-Like Evaluation
    of Conversational AI

    Evaluating conversational AI requires moving beyond static benchmarks toward methods that capture the subjective and multi-perspective ways humans judge dialogue. In this research area, I aim to design human-like evaluation frameworks that integrate diverse perspectives and assess conversational quality beyond surface-level correctness.
